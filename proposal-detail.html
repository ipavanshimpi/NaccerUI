Abstract

This research proposes an innovative AI-based system for automated coal quality assessment using advanced machine learning and computer vision techniques. The system aims to revolutionize traditional coal quality testing methods by providing real-time, accurate, and non-destructive analysis of coal samples. By leveraging deep learning algorithms and spectroscopic imaging, the proposed solution will significantly reduce assessment time from hours to minutes while improving accuracy by 25%. The system will be designed to integrate seamlessly with existing mining operations and provide actionable insights for quality control and optimization.

Problem Statement

Current coal quality assessment methods are time-consuming, labor-intensive, and often require destructive testing of samples. Traditional laboratory analysis can take 4-6 hours per sample, creating bottlenecks in production and quality control processes. Additionally, manual assessment is prone to human error and lacks consistency across different operators and facilities. The coal mining industry urgently needs an automated, accurate, and rapid quality assessment solution that can operate in real-time at mining sites without requiring extensive sample preparation or specialized laboratory equipment.

Research Objectives

  • Develop a deep learning model capable of analyzing coal quality parameters with 95% accuracy
  • Create a portable, field-deployable system for on-site coal quality assessment
  • Reduce assessment time from 4-6 hours to under 5 minutes per sample
  • Implement computer vision algorithms for automated sample analysis
  • Design user-friendly interface for operators with minimal technical training
  • Validate system performance across different coal types and mining conditions

Methodology

The research will employ a multi-phase approach combining machine learning, computer vision, and spectroscopic analysis. Phase 1 will focus on data collection, gathering a comprehensive dataset of coal samples with known quality parameters from various mines across India. Phase 2 will involve developing and training convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for image-based quality assessment, utilizing transfer learning from pre-trained models. Phase 3 will integrate spectroscopic sensors with the imaging system to capture additional chemical composition data. Phase 4 will focus on system integration, developing a portable hardware platform with embedded AI processing capabilities. The final phase will involve extensive field testing and validation across multiple mining sites, with iterative refinement based on real-world performance data.

Expected Outcomes

The project will deliver a fully functional AI-based coal quality assessment system capable of real-time analysis with 95% accuracy. Key deliverables include: (1) Trained machine learning models for coal quality prediction, (2) Portable hardware prototype with integrated sensors and processing unit, (3) User-friendly software interface with data visualization and reporting capabilities, (4) Comprehensive validation report demonstrating system performance across different coal types, (5) Technical documentation and user manuals, (6) At least 2 peer-reviewed publications in international journals, (7) Patent application for the novel assessment methodology. The system is expected to reduce quality assessment costs by 60% and improve operational efficiency in coal mining operations.
